## 1. Two Improvement Mechanisms (Ch8)

Companies improve through exactly two mechanisms:

### Best Practices Replication (复制最佳实践)
- Find the best performer (internal or external benchmark)
- Understand WHY they're the best (analyze their process)
- Replicate across the organization through standardization
- This is the fastest, cheapest way to improve

### Special Projects / PDCA Projects (专项改进)
- When no existing best practice can close the gap
- Requires new knowledge acquisition
- Uses full PDCA cycle: Plan → Do → Check → Act
- More resource-intensive but necessary for breakthrough improvements

**Always try replication first.** Only use special projects when the gap can't be closed
with existing knowledge.

## 2. Value Creation Model (Ch8, 图8-3)

Company value = Revenue - Costs - Capital employed

Three levers for improvement:
1. **Increase revenue** — Volume × Price × Mix optimization
2. **Reduce costs** — Operating costs + Indirect costs
3. **Optimize capital** — Working capital (especially inventory) + Fixed assets

## 3. Cost Management (Ch8)

### Cost Classification

**Operating costs (运营成本)** — directly related to production volume:
- Variable: raw materials, packaging, direct labor, energy for production
- Fixed: lighting, equipment operators, maintenance base cost

**Indirect costs (间接成本)** — support activities, not tied to production volume:
- Administration, HR, IT, finance, legal
- Often the "hidden" cost problem — grows unnoticed

### Porter's Value Chain for Cost Analysis
Map costs along the value chain:
Inbound logistics → Operations → Outbound logistics → Marketing & Sales → Service

For each stage, identify:
- What activities consume the most resources?
- Which activities add value vs. which are waste?
- Where are the biggest gaps vs. benchmarks?

### Cost Reduction Principles

For **operating costs**:
- Benchmark each cost element against best-in-class
- Standardize processes to reduce variation (variation = cost)
- Eliminate the three losses: waste (muda), inconsistency (mura), overburden (muri)
- Focus on raw material yield and energy efficiency

For **indirect costs**:
- Question every support activity: does it add value?
- Consolidate, automate, or eliminate where possible
- Benchmark administrative cost ratios against industry

## 4. Revenue Management (Ch8)

Revenue improvement requires understanding three dimensions:
- **Volume (销量)**: Can we sell more units? To whom? Through what channels?
- **Price (价格)**: Can we capture more value? Premium positioning? Better negotiation?
- **Mix (产品组合)**: Can we shift toward higher-margin products/services?

For each dimension:
1. Set a gap (benchmark vs. current)
2. Analyze the factors driving the gap
3. Create specific action plans with PDCA

## 5. Working Capital Management (Ch8)

### Inventory = The Key Lever
Toyota's zero-inventory philosophy: inventory hides problems. Reducing inventory:
- Exposes process weaknesses (forces you to fix them)
- Frees cash
- Reduces storage costs, obsolescence, damage

### Working Capital Formula
Working capital = Accounts receivable + Inventory - Accounts payable

Improve by:
- Reducing days of inventory (biggest lever)
- Reducing days sales outstanding (collect faster)
- Extending days payable (negotiate longer terms)

## 6. The PDCA Improvement Cycle in Detail (Ch8)

### P — Plan (计划)
1. **Define the problem clearly** with data (gap = ideal - current)
2. **Set a target** (~50% of the gap is a good starting point)
3. **Analyze root causes** using the analysis framework (see analysis-framework.md)
4. **Create an action plan**: WHO does WHAT by WHEN with what RESOURCES

Planning Principles:
- Analysis + Synthesis: break down, then reassemble into a coherent plan
- Focus + Participation: small analysis team, broad implementation team
- Prioritization + Optimization: 80/20 rule — focus on the vital few

### D — Do (执行)
This is where most organizations fail. Execution requires:
- Clear accountability: one person owns each action item
- Regular check-ins: weekly minimum
- Removing obstacles: leaders must clear the path
- Training: people must know HOW to execute

Without execution, the entire PDCA cycle is worthless.

### C — Check (验证)
- Compare results against the target
- Verify at regular intervals, not just at the end
- Use the same metrics defined in Plan
- If the target is met → proceed to Act
- If not met → analyze why and return to Plan

Verification is the most neglected step. Leaders must build a culture of checking.

### A — Act (处置)
Two paths:
- **Target met** → Standardize the new process (transition to SDCA)
- **Target not met** → Analyze what went wrong, revise the plan, cycle again

## 7. Execution: Making It Happen (Ch8)

### Why Execution Fails
1. Action plans are vague ("improve quality" instead of "reduce defect rate on Line 3 from 5% to 2% by Q3")
2. No single owner — shared accountability = no accountability
3. No regular follow-up mechanism
4. Leaders don't remove obstacles
5. People weren't trained on the new method

### Falconi's Execution Principles
- Every action item has ONE owner and a deadline
- Follow up weekly at minimum
- The leader's job is to ensure execution happens (see 13-item leadership system in leadership-knowledge.md)
- Celebrate progress; address non-performance directly

## 8. Verification: The Critical "C" (Ch8)

### What to Verify
- Are action items being completed on time?
- Are the metrics moving in the right direction?
- Are the root causes actually being addressed?
- Is the new way of working being followed?

### How to Verify
- Go to where the work happens (Gemba / 现地现物)
- Look at the data, not just reports
- Talk to the people doing the work
- Compare actual vs. planned at every review

### Leadership Accountability
The leader who set the goal is accountable for verifying progress.
Delegation of execution is fine; delegation of verification is abdication.
