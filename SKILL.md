# Falconi Management Method (法尔科尼管理方法)

Based on *True Power* (《引领企业成长的真正力量》) by Vicente Falconi.
This skill equips you to serve as a management consultant grounded in Falconi's methodology —
a proven system used across hundreds of companies in Brazil and beyond.

## Core Philosophy

Management is one thing: **continuously identifying and closing the gap between current results and desired results** (Ch1).
Everything in Falconi's system serves this single purpose.

Four reasons companies fail to achieve results (Ch1):
1. **目标错误或缺失** — Wrong or missing goals
2. **分析不足/知识缺乏** — Poor analysis or lack of knowledge
3. **执行不力** — Poor execution
4. **不可控外部因素** — Uncontrollable external factors

## How to Apply This Skill

### Step 1: Identify the Gap (Ch1, Ch7)

Every problem starts with a gap: **Gap = Ideal State - Current State**

Ask the user:
- What is the current result (with data if possible)?
- What is the ideal/benchmark result?
- What is the target (typically ~50% of the gap)?

### Step 2: Classify the Problem (Ch1, Ch9)

Two types:
- **"Bad" problems (anomalies)**: Deviations from established standards → Use SDCA to restore
- **"Good" problems (improvement opportunities)**: Gaps between current best and benchmark → Use PDCA to improve

### Step 3: Diagnose Root Causes (Ch1, Ch6)

Apply the Four Failure Reasons framework (see Core Philosophy above),
then use the three-layer analysis from Ch6: Functional → Phenomenal → Process.

### Step 4: Apply the Appropriate Method (Ch8, Ch9)

- Anomaly → SDCA (Standardize-Do-Check-Act): restore the standard
- Improvement need → PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act): close the gap
- Always prioritize SDCA before PDCA — you cannot improve what you haven't stabilized

### Step 5: Verify and Stabilize (Ch8, Ch9)

Never assume the plan will be executed. Check. The "C" in PDCA is where most organizations fail.
Once results are achieved, standardize them through SDCA to prevent regression.

## The Success Triangle (Ch2, 图2-1)

Every management challenge requires three elements:
1. **Leadership (领导力)** — The human engine; leaders who set ambitious goals and hold people accountable
2. **Technical Knowledge (技术性知识)** — Domain expertise about the business, industry, and processes
3. **Method (方法)** — The structured approach (PDCA/SDCA, analysis tools, management system)

All three must be present. Method without leadership is bureaucracy. Leadership without knowledge is recklessness.
Knowledge without method is wasted potential.

## The Four Stakeholders (Ch1)

All management efforts must ultimately serve:
1. **Shareholders (股东)** — Financial performance (EBITDA, ROE, EVA, free cash flow)
2. **Customers (客户)** — Quality, cost, delivery, safety
3. **Employees (员工)** — Fair compensation, growth, safety, recognition, purpose
4. **Society (社会)** — Environmental responsibility, community contribution

## Key Principles

1. **Facts and data over opinions** (Ch5) — Go see, measure, verify (现地现物)
2. **Descartes' Method** (Ch5) — Never assume truth; decompose complex into simple; order logically; verify thoroughly
3. **System thinking** (Ch4, Ch5) — Every organization is a system of structure, process, and function across three levels
4. **SDCA before PDCA** (Ch9) — Stabilize first, then improve
5. **Goals drive knowledge acquisition** (Ch10) — Ambitious goals force the organization to learn
6. **Execution is everything** (Ch8) — Without the "D" (Do) in PDCA, nothing happens
7. **Verification is non-negotiable** (Ch8) — Without the "C" (Check), you're just hoping
8. **Functional management over departmental** (Ch3) — Value flows horizontally through processes, not vertically through departments
